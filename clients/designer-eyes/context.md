# Designer Eyes — Project Context

## About the Brand

Designer Eyes (designereyes.com) is a **luxury authorized eyewear retailer** (sunglasses + optical) with an omnichannel model: ecommerce + 4 physical boutiques in the two highest-value affluent Latino/LATAM-gateway corridors of the U.S. territory.

- **Boutiques:**
  - Brickell City Centre (Miami, FL)
  - Aventura Mall (Aventura, FL)
  - San Juan, Puerto Rico
  - Plaza Las Américas (San Juan, PR)
- **Customer Service:** 877-720-8393 · cs@designereyes.com · Mon–Fri 9am–5pm EDT
- **Platform:** Shopify (myshopify.com backend)

## Positioning

Multi-brand **luxury authorized retailer** targeting an affluent, design-literate buyer — heavy skew toward U.S.–LATAM elite, Miami locals, and international tourists. The brand's claim "*a luxury eyewear retailer. Our vast selection of sunglasses and eyeglasses features styles that are exclusive to us*" anchors curation and exclusivity as the core differentiator.

- **Price band:** ~$400–$1,000+ retail (most SKUs $450–$700)
- **Category mix:** Sunglasses, Optical, Prescription, Blue Light, Iconic/Archive
- **Services:** Prescription lenses, blue light, designer frame fitting

## Brand Portfolio (30+ authorized)

**Top brands:** Cartier, DIOR, Jacques Marie Mage, Fendi, Tom Ford, Loewe, Prada, Miu Miu, Gucci, Celine, Movitra, Akoni

**All brands:** Balmain, Carrera, Cartier, Celine, Costa Del Mar, Dior, Dita, Dolce&Gabbana, Fendi, Gucci, Jacques Marie Mage, Loewe, Maui Jim, Miu Miu, Montblanc, Movitra, Mykita, Oakley, Oliver Peoples, Prada, Prada Linea Rossa, Ray-Ban, Ray-Ban Kids, Saint Laurent, Silhouette, Tag Heuer, Tiffany & Co., Tom Ford, Versace

## Target Market & Competitive Set

- **Geography:** US (FL + PR core), with heavy LATAM tourist overlap.
- **Ticket:** $450–$800 average SKU; halo SKUs $1,000–$3,000 (Cartier, Chrome Hearts-adjacent, Jacques Marie Mage).
- **Competitors analyzed:** 10 direct & indirect, organized in 4 tiers (see `memory/competitors.md`).

## Strategic Priorities (from competitor analysis)

1. **Double down on "Miami-Latino Luxury" positioning** — bilingual ES/EN editorial, loyalty/VIC, SJU + MIA as gateway boutiques for LATAM HNW tourists.
2. **Monetize boutique experience via VIC program** — tiered concierge, private appointments, early Cartier/Dior drops, home try-on.
3. **Out-assort Luxottica/Safilo chains with niche authorized brands** — Jacques Marie Mage, Chrome Hearts, Dita, Mykita, Lindberg, Kuboraum, Linda Farrow.
4. **Own the Rx/lens story as a luxury service** — Zeiss/Essilor/Rodenstock premium tiers, lifetime adjustments, margin + retention play.
5. **Geographic expansion** — Dallas (NorthPark), Houston (Galleria), Atlanta (Lenox), NYC (SoHo) in 24 months; hotel/private-aviation partnerships to intercept affluent travelers.

## Main Threats (summary)

- **Luxottica consolidation** (Sunglass Hut + LensCrafters, owns Oakley/Ray-Ban + Prada/Versace/Burberry licenses) → margin + allocation squeeze.
- **Brand-direct DTC** (Cartier, Dior, Gucci, Prada own ecommerce) → brands increasingly pulling inventory from wholesale.
- **Luxury department store flagships in Bal Harbour** (Saks, Neiman) head-to-head with Aventura/Brickell boutiques.
- **Grey market / parallel imports** eroding pricing integrity in LATAM.
- **DTC digital-native players** (Shades Eyeconic, FramesDirect) eroding aspirational luxury from below on SEO/PPC.

## SWOT (condensed)

- **Strengths:** curated luxury portfolio · 4 boutiques in highest-value Latino corridors · exclusive SKUs · authorized across Kering + LVMH + Richemont · boutique service.
- **Weaknesses:** concentrated footprint (FL + PR only) · much smaller store count vs Sunglass Hut/LensCrafters · weaker brand awareness outside MIA/PR · Rx ops less developed.
- **Opportunities:** expansion to TX/NY/ATL · Rx/lens premium service · bilingual ES/EN content · loyalty/VIC · pre-owned resale · hotel partnerships for tourist flows.
- **Threats:** Luxottica · brand DTC · department store luxury · grey market · tech parity required (AI try-on).
