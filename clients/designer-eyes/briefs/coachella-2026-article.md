## How to Choose the Right Sunglasses for a Festival

The most important criterion is one that gets overlooked in the excitement of aesthetics: UV protection. Any frame you wear at Coachella should offer 100% UVA and UVB protection — the desert sun at peak hours is genuinely intense, and cumulative UV exposure is a real concern over a multi-day event. At Designer Eyes, every frame in the collection meets this standard, but it is worth confirming on any purchase regardless of where you shop.

Beyond UV protection, fit and durability matter more at a festival than in everyday contexts. Frames that slide down your nose in humidity, or that feel uncomfortable after two hours, will spend the afternoon in your bag rather than on your face. Look for frames with appropriate nose pad adjustability and temple arm length for your face. Acetate frames tend to wear comfortably over long periods; metal frames offer durability and lightness.

Style cohesion is the final consideration. Your sunglasses should feel like they belong to the same world as your outfit — not necessarily matching in color or material, but sharing an energy and intention. A sculptural architectural frame reads differently against a boho layered look than it does against clean minimalism, and understanding that relationship is what separates a great festival look from a good one.

Investing in a designer frame for a recurring event like Coachella makes straightforward sense: cost-per-wear decreases every time you reach for them, and a well-made frame from a house like Loewe or Cartier will be as wearable and relevant next season as it is this one.
