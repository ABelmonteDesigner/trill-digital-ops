## Tier 1 — Dominant US omnichannel chains

### 1. Sunglass Hut (Primary threat)
- **URL / Founded:** sunglasshut.com/us · 1971
- **Avg price:** ~$170 ($80 – $500+)
- **Sun brands (3rd party):** Ray-Ban, Oakley, Prada, Versace, Dolce & Gabbana
- **Optical brands (3rd party):** Coach, Burberry, Tiffany & Co., Michael Kors, Gucci
- **Standout:**
  - Luxottica monopoly advantage — owns Ray-Ban and Oakley outright; exclusive deals competitors can't match.
  - 1,700+ US touchpoints including every major airport and shopping center.
  - In-store services: same-day lens replacement, frame engraving, nose-pad adjustments.
  - Google Shopping king across premium sunglasses queries with massive ad budget.
  - Hinge program: buy any frame + replacement lenses at a discount — drives repeat visits.

### 2. LensCrafters (Adjacent threat)
- **URL / Founded:** lenscrafters.com · 1983
- **Avg price:** ~$190 ($100 – $400)
- **Sun brands:** Ray-Ban, Oakley, Persol, Coach, Vogue Eyewear
- **Optical brands:** Ralph Lauren, Michael Kors, Prada, Dolce & Gabbana, Kate Spade
- **Standout:**
  - In-store optometrist — eye exam, prescription, and sunglass purchase in one visit.
  - Accepts VSP, EyeMed, Cigna, Aetna.
  - AccuFit digital facial measurement.
  - On-site lab with 1-hour lens crafting at most locations.
  - 2-year guarantee (scratch + no-fault breakage).

### 10. Visionworks (Optical chain w/ ecommerce)
- **URL / Founded:** visionworks.com · 1988
- **Avg price:** ~$150 ($60 – $350)
- **Sun brands:** Ray-Ban, Oakley, Coach, Maui Jim, Prada
- **Optical brands:** Kate Spade, Versace, Michael Kors, Polo Ralph Lauren, Vogue Eyewear
- **Standout:**
  - Signature BOGO promotions on complete pairs.
  - 700+ US locations across 44 states — strong in suburbs where Sunglass Hut is thinner.
  - Insurance: VSP, EyeMed, Davis Vision, Cigna, United Healthcare.
  - In-store eye exams with optometrists.
  - Online booking integrated with in-store fulfillment (click-to-brick).

## Tier 2 — Online-first US eyewear retailers

### 3. Warby Parker (Direct DTC threat)
- **URL / Founded:** warbyparker.com · 2010
- **Avg price:** ~$115 ($95 – $145)
- **Brands:** Own/private label exclusively.
- **Standout:**
  - Home Try-On: 5 frames shipped free for 5 days — removes the #1 online barrier.
  - Best-in-class AR virtual try-on (iPhone face-mapping).
  - Buy a Pair, Give a Pair social mission.
  - Every SKU available in prescription.
  - Proprietary seasonal design drops — anticipation without discounting.

### 4. EyeBuyDirect (SEO threat)
- **URL / Founded:** eyebuydirect.com · 2006
- **Avg price:** ~$35 ($6 – $90)
- **Brands:** Own/private label exclusively.
- **Standout:**
  - One of the highest DA eyewear sites in the US — dominates budget queries.
  - $6 entry point — lowest price floor of any branded US online eyewear retailer.
  - Frequent BOGO promotions.
  - EBDBlue proprietary blue-light coating (health-benefit positioning).
  - Every sunglass frame available with Rx lenses.

### 5. Zenni Optical (Price threat)
- **URL / Founded:** zennioptical.com · 2003
- **Avg price:** ~$22 ($7 – $50)
- **Brands:** Own brand only.
- **Standout:**
  - $7 starting price — created the "why pay $200 at the mall?" consumer mindset.
  - Blokz blue-light tech (screen workers, gamers) drives search traffic.
  - SnapMag magnetic clip-on sun lens system — upsell driver.
  - Early AI virtual try-on — normalized online frame buying.
  - Tens of millions of US repeat Rx customers in email database for fashion upsell.

## Tier 3 — Specialty & multi-brand ecommerce

### 6. GlassesUSA (Designer discount threat)
- **URL / Founded:** glassesusa.com · 2009
- **Avg price:** ~$95 ($38 – $300+)
- **Sun brands:** Ray-Ban, Tom Ford, Gucci, Prada, Versace
- **Optical brands:** Oakley, Coach, Burberry, Kate Spade, Michael Kors
- **Standout:**
  - Up to 70% off designer retail — most aggressive discounting in US online.
  - 150+ brands, 10,000+ styles — competes with Sunglass Hut w/o mall overhead.
  - Virtual try-on + face shape guide (above-average AR).
  - 365-day return window — industry-leading.
  - Free shipping + free extra pair of lenses on most orders.

### 7. Frames Direct (Sport + premium online)
- **URL / Founded:** framesdirect.com · 1996
- **Avg price:** ~$185 ($50 – $500+)
- **Sun brands:** Ray-Ban, Maui Jim, Costa del Mar, Oakley, Wiley X
- **Optical brands:** Tom Ford, Smith Optics, Serengeti, Bolle, Kaenon
- **Standout:**
  - Pioneer — one of the first US online eyewear retailers (1996). Near-30-year SEO authority.
  - Only major US retailer covering sport (Costa, Wiley X) + luxury (Tom Ford) in one catalog.
  - Price match guarantee vs. Sunglass Hut's fixed pricing.
  - SmartBuyGlasses partnership — covers all price tiers.
  - Certified opticians via phone/chat for Rx fitting.

### 8. SportRx (Sport niche)
- **URL / Founded:** sportrx.com · 2009
- **Avg price:** ~$200 ($80 – $400+)
- **Sun brands:** Oakley, Costa del Mar, Maui Jim, Smith Optics, Wiley X
- **Optical brands:** Nike Vision, Bolle, Tifosi, Kaenon, Dragon Alliance
- **Standout:**
  - US leader in prescription sport sunglasses; in-house certified lab.
  - Sport-specific lens customization (cycling, golf, water, snow).
  - Free 2-way shipping (outbound + returns).
  - Deep active lifestyle content (blog + YouTube) drives organic traffic.
  - Authorized dealer for all major performance brands.

### 9. Clearly (Online multi-brand)
- **URL / Founded:** clearly.com · 2000
- **Avg price:** ~$78 ($20 – $300)
- **Sun brands:** Ray-Ban, Oakley, Maui Jim, Tom Ford, Gucci
- **Optical brands:** Coach, Kate Spade, Saint Laurent, Prada, Versace
- **Standout:**
  - Best-in-class ecommerce UX (product pages, filtering, checkout).
  - High-quality AR virtual try-on with face-shape detection.
  - Broad price ladder $20–$300 — retains buyer across life stages.
  - Free shipping threshold $99+ (AOV driver from avg $78).
  - Frequent 30–50% off promotions driving email engagement.

## SWOT — Shades Eyeconic vs. full US field

### Strengths
- No physical retail overhead — full DTC margins on every sale.
- Ownable aesthetic identity — not a reseller of others' brands.
- Agility — drop new styles faster than chains or multi-brand retailers.
- Direct customer relationship — owned data, no middleman.

### Weaknesses
- No physical try-on vs. Sunglass Hut / LensCrafters / Visionworks.
- Brand awareness far below all 10 listed competitors.
- SEO domain authority gap vs. EyeBuyDirect, Clearly, Zenni.
- No home try-on program (Warby Parker is the benchmark).

### Opportunities
- The $50–$90 fashion DTC tier has no dominant identity-led player.
- TikTok Shop — fastest-growing channel for impulse sunglass purchases.
- SEO content gap: style guides, seasonal drops, face shape guides.
- Micro-influencer affiliate program at low CAC.

### Threats
- GlassesUSA + Clearly aggressively discounting designer names.
- EyeBuyDirect + Zenni dominating budget organic search.
- Sunglass Hut monopolizing Google Shopping ad positions.
- Warby Parker expanding sunglasses catalog into new price tiers.

## Top 5 strategic recommendations

1. **Own the $50–$90 fashion DTC positioning.** No dominant identity-led brand sits between Warby Parker ($95+) and EyeBuyDirect (sub-$35). Every communication should reinforce: quality sunglasses, distinct aesthetic, no mall required.
2. **Build an SEO content moat vs. EyeBuyDirect & Clearly.** Long-tail queries like "best sunglasses for oval face women USA," "trendy sunglasses 2026," "festival sunglasses summer." 2–3 editorial posts/month targeting the gap.
3. **Launch home try-on or AR virtual try-on (Warby benchmark).** The biggest online sunglasses barrier is fit uncertainty. A 5-frames-at-home or quality AR tool neutralizes the physical-retail advantage of Sunglass Hut and LensCrafters.
4. **Activate TikTok Shop before competitors.** Sunglass Hut and specialty retailers are slow there. The $50–$90 fashion impulse buy is perfectly sized for TikTok Shop. First-mover advantage still available.
5. **Build trust signals to beat GlassesUSA's discount model.** GlassesUSA trains customers to wait for 60% off. Shades Eyeconic wins via brand value, authentic UGC, creator content, and an origin story — reasons to pay full price.
